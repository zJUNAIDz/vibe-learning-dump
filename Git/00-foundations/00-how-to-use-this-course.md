
# How to Use This Course: A Production Engineering Approach to Git

- **Purpose**: To set the stage for the course, explaining its philosophy, structure, and what makes it different from typical Git tutorials.
- **Estimated Difficulty**: 1/5
- **Estimated Reading Time**: 20 minutes
- **Prerequisites**: None

---

### 1. Course Philosophy

Welcome to the Git Supercurriculum. This is not a beginner's guide. It's a deep dive for experienced developers who want to move from casual user to genuine expert. We will treat Git not as a simple version control tool, but as what it truly is:

- A **distributed database** for source code.
- A **content-addressable object store**.
- A **directed acyclic graph (DAG)** management system.
- A **filesystem snapshot engine**.

Our focus is on building robust mental models that allow you to reason about Git's behavior from first principles. We will embrace complexity, explore internals, and simulate production disasters. The goal is not just to learn commands, but to understand the *why* behind them and the system-level mechanics at play.

### 2. Who This Course Is For

This curriculum is designed for you if:
- You have used Git for years but lack confidence in high-stakes scenarios.
- You've worked mostly solo or on small teams and want to master collaborative workflows.
- You are tired of memorizing commands and want to understand Git's internal data model.
- You want to be the person on your team who can confidently fix any Git problem.

### 3. Course Structure

The curriculum is divided into modules, each building on the last.

- **00-Foundations**: Sets the stage and establishes our core mental models.
- **01-Git-Internals**: The heart of the course. We dissect the object database.
- **02-Advanced-Local-Workflows**: Mastering your local repository.
- **03-Collaboration-and-Remotes**: The distributed nature of Git.
- **04-History-Rewriting**: The art and ethics of changing the past.
- **05-Recovery-and-Debugging**: Your survival guide for when things go wrong.
- **06-Branching-Strategies**: Architecting workflows for teams.
- **07-Scaling-Git**: Handling large repositories and performance.
- **08-Team-Dynamics**: The human side of version control.
- **09-Tooling**: Enhancing your Git productivity.
- **10-Appendices**: A reference for plumbing commands, glossaries, and more.
- **11-Capstone-Projects**: Real-world simulations.
- **12-Interview-Preparation**: Solidifying your knowledge for job interviews.

### 4. How to Learn

- **Read Actively**: Don't just skim. Type out the commands. Break them. Fix them.
- **Do the Labs**: The labs are designed to be challenging. They will force you to apply concepts under pressure.
- **Embrace the Terminal**: While GUIs are useful, this course is terminal-first to ensure you understand the underlying mechanics.
- **Visualize**: Use the provided Mermaid diagrams and draw your own commit graphs. Thinking in graphs is the key to Git mastery.
- **Simulate**: Use `git-supercurriculum/labs` to create safe spaces for the destructive exercises.

### 5. A Note on Difficulty

This course is intentionally difficult. You will be pushed out of your comfort zone. You will encounter frustrating problems. This is by design. Production environments are unforgiving, and the goal is to prepare you for them. Stick with it, and you will emerge with a level of Git expertise few developers ever achieve.
