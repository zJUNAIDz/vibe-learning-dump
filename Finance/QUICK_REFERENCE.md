## The 60-Second Financial Plan

```
1. Emergency Fund     → 6 months expenses in liquid fund / savings
2. Health Insurance   → ₹10-20L cover, OWN policy (not just employer's)
3. Term Insurance     → 10× annual income (only if dependents)
4. SIP in Index Fund  → 20%+ of take-home, auto-debit Day 1
5. PPF               → ₹1.5L/year (max out 80C)
6. NPS               → ₹50K/year (extra 80CCD deduction)
7. File ITR           → Every year, on time
8. Increase SIP 10%  → Every year when salary grows
```

## Tax: Old vs New Regime (FY 2025-26)

```
Income ≤ ₹12L?     → New Regime (zero tax with rebate)
Income ₹12-20L?    → Compare both. New usually wins.
Income > ₹20L with
  home loan + HRA?  → Old Regime may win. Calculate both.

When in doubt: Use ClearTax/Tax2Win regime comparison tool.
```

### Old Regime Key Deductions
- **80C:** PPF, ELSS, EPF, life insurance — ₹1.5L limit
- **80D:** Health insurance — ₹25K self + ₹25-50K parents
- **80CCD(1B):** NPS — ₹50K additional
- **24(b):** Home loan interest — ₹2L limit
- **HRA:** Based on rent, salary, city

## ESOP Quick Check

```
Before valuing ESOPs, ask:
  1. What's the last funding valuation?
  2. What's the strike/exercise price?
  3. What's the vesting schedule + cliff?
  4. Is there a liquidity path (IPO/buyback)?
  5. What's the dilution risk?
  6. What are the tax implications on exercise?

Rule of thumb: Value ESOPs at ₹0 until liquidity event.
Negotiate salary as if ESOPs don't exist.
```

## Financial Independence (FIRE) Quick Math

```
Step 1: Monthly expenses × 12 = Annual expenses
Step 2: Annual expenses × 25 = FI Number
Step 3: FI Number ÷ withdrawal rate (3.5%) = Sustainable income

Example:
  ₹1L/month × 12 = ₹12L/year
  ₹12L × 25 = ₹3 Crores
  ₹3 Cr × 3.5% = ₹10.5L/year passive income
```
